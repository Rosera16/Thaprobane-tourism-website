Trincomalee

Trincomalee is one of the historical towns on the east coast of Sri Lanka , offers beautiful white sand and is one of the best out of all beaches.

The east coast is upcoming and way more quiet than south, which is a perfect reason to plan a visit for tourists and locals.

Trincomalee has one of the world's finest natural harbors, accompanied with a deep water port which has been the reason for several attacks in the past.

It has housed some of the finest beaches in its ambit such as Nilaveli, Uppuveli and Pigeon Island and ideal location for Whale watching.

Rarely a Blue Whale would be in sight near Swami Rock which is considered to be the greatest vantage point.

Nilaveli Beach

Nilaveli, a town in the coast of Sri Lanka, is a very well-known place amongst the tourists, for those who frequently visit the pearl of the Indian Ocean.

The area gets its name from it’s tamil origin, who are considered to be amongst the first settlers in the area, dating back to the 10th Century.

The city is still largely a Tamil populated city, and was one of the most affected cities by the 2004 tsunami.

This picturesque town just 16 Km away from the city of Trincomalee is considered a tourist attraction,as it has a lot of unique activities within its city limits.

Nilaveli Beach is one of the finest beaches in Sri lanka, the pearly sands, the breath-taking coastline this is a tangible proof of serenity in our island.In the weekend, locals from all over the country also like to visit Nilaveli because of its white sand.

This is a place where most people come to take a break to enjoy with their close ones.

Pigeon Island

The Pigeon Island, floating in the great blue 1km offshore contains over 100 species of stunning coral and is teeming with over 300 species of colourful reef fish, including the Black Tip Reef Shark, a variety of rays, and Sri Lanka’s iconic sea turtle.

The coral reef is about 200m long and 100m in width approximately.

And It is the base for hundreds of sea creatures living around.

With its powdery white sands and glittering coral gardens, tantalises with possibilities,

The rocky outcrops and the islets is a breeding and feeding ground for over 300 species of coral reef fish keeping the colorful underwater symphony alive.

It is considered as an ideal spot for snorkeling since the reef is shallow.

Whale watching

Whale and dolphin watching in Sri Lanka is one of the island’s biggest seasonal attractions.

The waters off the coast of Jungle Beach offer some of the best spots in the world to catch a glimpse of the world’s largest ever mammal known as the Blue Whale.

Furthermore, the deepest diving whales and the largest predators on Earth,the Sperm Whales are also plentiful in these parts and are often spotted traveling in super pods of 40 or more.

These bountiful waters are also feeding grounds for large numbers of Spinner Dolphins who are known for their joyous acrobatic displays.

Kanniya 7 wells

Located at Anuradhapura - Trincomalee road, the Kanniya hot water springs are a natural marvel that are intertwined with the myth and legends of Sri Lanka according to the locals in the area.

There are seven wells each, about 3 - 4 feet deep and all are built in a square shape.

Hundreds of travellers both locals and tourists, Due to its popularity, the kanniya springs are crowded during the day which makes it difficult for everyone to enjoy the beauty and spirituality of the surroundings and experience.

Travellers who are determined to soothe their bodies and mind ,come here to experience it by the warmth of the natural spring water from the 7 wells of kanniya.
